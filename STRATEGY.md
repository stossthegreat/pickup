## What Users Actually Hate

All complaints below pulled from App Store, Google Play, JustUseApp, Trustpilot, Reddit, Looksmax.org, and journalism aggregators. Verbatim quotes preserved.

### Looks Apps — Top 5 Cross-App Complaints

#### 1. Scores randomize on the same face (Universal)
The category-defining methodology failure.

> *"Submitted the same picture 3 times and almost every time received a different number."* — Umax user
>
> *"Uploading multiple photos with different expressions resulted in the exact same feedback. Same pose, 6.7 to 8.4."* — LooksMax AI user
>
> *"All these 'AI' rating sites are usually just analyzing symmetry which is only one of many features vital to face attractiveness."* — Looksmax.org community thread

**Frequency:** ~25–30% of negative reviews for both LooksMax AI and Umax.

#### 2. Paywall AFTER photo upload — dark pattern (Universal)
> *"They wait until after you submit all your photos to tell you it's actually a subscription service."*
>
> *"Umax takes the photos that users put in and then holds them on a paywall, not letting users remove their photos and forcing them to either pay for a weekly subscription or delete the app."*

**Frequency:** LooksMax AI 35–40% / Umax 40–50% of negative reviews. This is the single most-mentioned grievance in the looks category.

#### 3. Single number, zero explanation (Umax #1 wished feature)
> *"Doesn't give details on why that is or what it means… if the app went more in depth and possibly cropped the photo and zoomed in to show details, that would be helpful."*

**Implication:** Users want annotated overlays on their own face — the exact data ImHim already computes.

#### 4. Generic ChatGPT-wrapper advice (Universal)
> *"The advice is complete BS and the AI is worthless… same advice regardless of whether you're smiling or trying to look bad."* — LooksMax AI
>
> *"Vague tips… drinking more water or lowering your body fat percentage. Analysis without action, zero practical tools."* — Umax

**The proof:** Umax founder Blake Anderson publicly confirmed GPT-4 Vision is the entire backend. There's no model moat anywhere in the category.

#### 5. Pay-on-pay (LooksMax distinctive)
> *"$5 for AI pictures of different hair styles that disappear when you close the app."*

LooksMax AI sub is $9.99/wk; hairstyle simulator is an extra $5; the generated hairstyles vanish on close. Users pay twice and lose the result both times.

#### Bonus signal: Ethnic feature blindness
> *"User with extremely narrow eyes (like Asians) was analyzed as having round eyes. Diamond face shape labeled square."* — Umax

MIT's Joy Buolamwini research confirms the pattern: commercial face AI misidentifies darker-skinned features at far higher rates. None of these apps adjust for ethnicity.

### Rizz Apps — Top 5 Cross-App Complaints

#### 1. AI can't tell who sent what in the screenshot (Wing #1)
> *"Doesn't understand who is sending vs who is receiving messages when you send a screenshot of a chat between you and the person you like."*

**Frequency:** 30–40% of Wing AI negative reviews. Most-common single rizz complaint.

#### 2. Generic, cringe replies (Universal)
> *"Every time they ask for a pickup line it always starts with 'Hey, are you a magician?'"* — Rizz user
>
> *"The responses are like 50 year olds tryna text each other love messages… embarrassingly cheesy."*
>
> *"It's not that clever, tells you to say cringe things half the time. You can find a million generic pickup lines on Google and Reddit, most of which will be a lot better."* — Plug AI

**Frequency:** 25–30% across Rizz/Plug/Wing.

#### 3. No conversation memory (Rizz/Plug/Wing all)
> *"Didn't know 12 hours had passed, didn't know how to link previous talking points."*

Every screenshot treated as a brand-new conversation. Users want continuity.

#### 4. No profile-to-opener mode (Plug #1 wished feature)
> *"Needed to allow you to upload screenshots of a girl's profile so the AI can come up with a custom opener."*

Every app handles in-conversation screenshots; none generate from her bio.

#### 5. No multi-screenshot + no written context (Rizz #1 wished)
> *"Wait a full week before getting even 5 more replies. Multi-screenshot stitching for long convos, free-form context box: 'she's my coworker, we matched on Hinge, last date was 5 days ago.'"*

### Universal — Trust Failures (both categories, all 5 apps)

#### Cancel-hell + surprise auto-charge (5/5)
> *"Continue to be charged weekly… the email doesn't work."*
>
> *"Wrongfully charged $60 when trying to cancel."*

> *"It made me buy stuff even when I undownloaded the app… now I'm in trouble with my family bc we are poor."*

Umax ToS: "All purchases are non-refundable" except CA/CT residents. Apple policy doesn't actually let you contract out of statutory refund rights — this is one App Review pass away from a problem.

#### Inflated reviews via nag-rating (Rizz especially)
> *"Each time you press anything inside the app it reprompts to rate 5 stars, followed by an ad and then a paywall."*

Rizz claims 4.8★ on App Store. Trustpilot is 2.7★. The gap is the manipulation.

## RETENTION — The Real Moat

### Why competitors lose users in 2-3 weeks

Every one of these apps is a **NUMBER**. You take a selfie, you get a number, you close the app. Two weeks later you've memorized the number, the advice is the same generic loop, there's no progression, no community, no reason to come back. **Pure transactional curiosity, satisfied in 1-3 sessions.**

The data backs it up:
- **Umax** rate-limits to 1 scan/week and paywalls retakes — actively kicks users out
- **LooksMax AI** serves the same checklist on every photo — no reason to re-engage
- **Rizz** free tier is "5 replies/week" — users churn the moment they need more
- **All 5** ship zero habit-loop infrastructure — no daily ritual, no streak, no compound

App Annie / Sensor Tower data shows the curve: massive download spikes (TikTok virality), 80%+ Day 1 churn, MoM declines after Day 30. Umax went from #1 lifestyle to #200 in 6 months. There's no retention engine in this category. **That's the gap.**

### Why ImHim holds users — engineered retention infrastructure

#### 1. The ImHim Score — one number that compounds

Combine Looks (geometry + Vision) and Game (Free Flow score) into a single 0–100 ImHim Score.

- **Week 1:** 60% looks / 40% game (so day-1 users get a meaningful number from just a scan)
- **After 3 voice sessions:** flip to 50/50 (heavy users earn the game weight)
- **Home screen shows daily delta:** +0.3 since last week → up-arrow, score animates

**This is the killer differentiator: competitors give you a one-shot rating; we give you a number that moves.** Users come back to watch the number move. The number IS the product.

#### 2. 60-Day Protocols (shipped, underpromoted)

Skin, Jaw, Debloat, Hair — each is a 60-day commit with daily check-ins. Streaks compound. **Streaks are biological retention.** Casinos know this. Duolingo knows this. The looksmax category has never built it.

Promote it in App Store screenshots as the differentiator.

#### 3. Roleplay Leaderboards (game scores only — Apple-safe)

Two boards:
- **Weekly Top 100** — resets Mondays. Guaranteed weekly return for anyone in the top 1000.
- **All-Time Top 100** — status flex. Permanent identity in the app.

Anonymous handles by default (`CHAOS_RIDER_4421`). Optional display name unlock at level 5.

Critical: **never leaderboard looks scores.** That's Apple-rejection bait (Guideline 1.1.4 — comparison of personal attributes) and EU AI Act 2025 flagged use (biometric ranking). Game scores are a game metric — safe.

#### 4. Daily Coach Drop

7:30pm local push notification, one personalized line tied to the user's most-stale tab. The `DailyNudgeService` already exists; copy needs sharpening:

- No scan in 14d → *"Your last scan was 14 days ago. Re-scan tonight — see if mewing moved the needle."*
- No Free Flow in 7d → *"Your last roleplay was 7 days ago. Lucien's waiting."*
- Active streak → *"Day 23. Keep going."*
- New leaderboard rank → *"You climbed 12 positions overnight. Top 1.2%."*

#### 5. Killer Share Card — the viral retention loop

Pulls everything together onto one Twitter/IG-story shaped card:

- **ImHim Score** huge in top corner
- **Before/after** render OR hero shot
- **ONE coach line** tied to their breakdown — *"you look good, go next to her, stand there like a bro wall"* template
- **Tiny watermark** — `imhim.app · #47 this week`

Auto-generated whenever the score updates. One-tap share to IG / Twitter / iMessage.

**The loop that breaks the category:**

```
User shares card
  → friend sees friend's score increase
    → friend installs ImHim
      → friend's first scan → friend's first ImHim Score
        → friend shares THEIR card
          → compound
```

BeReal, Co-Star, Spotify Wrapped — every app with a killer share card hits explosive organic growth. **None of the looksmax or rizz competitors ship a share card.** This is uncontested territory.

#### 6. Wingman Mode (long-term moat)

When v1 is rock-solid: AI wingman that listens to your conversation through AirPods and whispers reply suggestions. Frame as "personal coach" not "surveillance" — explicit consent screen at session start, never store other-party audio.

Cluely (interview coach) and the meeting-AI category already proved Apple ships this pattern. We'd be first in the dating category. **Daily-use tool, not a weekly check-in. Massive retention play.**
